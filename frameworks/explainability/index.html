Any hypothesis or result should be supported by a base of confirmed (or at least plausible) mechanisms.

Building systems on a base of explainability has the added benefit of making easier and quicker to assess and understand discrepancies between hypotheses and results.

Being able to explain even highly complex systems, both in detail and at a high level, is also a demonstration of mastery and is something to strive for and seek out in others.

"Black box" systems, at best, expose us to risk. At worst, accepting unsubstantiated claims without scrutiny propagates extremism.
